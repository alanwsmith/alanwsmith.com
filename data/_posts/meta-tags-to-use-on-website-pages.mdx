### Notes

- These are copies of the `<head>` section/data from the various pages. 
- I removed `<script>` and `<style>` tags. Everything else has been left in place. 
- This is the content from the "Sources" tag in chrome's developer console (i.e. not what's in the "Elements" tab). This is based off the assumption that the various crawlers will primarily use the content directly from the file without further processing. 
- Looking at the Elements tab in the dev console there are some differences like styles being added to the `<html>` tag. Finding those differences is left as an exercise for the reader.
- Most of the pages actually had a line break after the text of the title and the closing `</title>` tag. Doesn't seem like that should be important, but it's interesting to see that they basically all have it. 
- Twitter really doesn't have a `<head>` tag or a `<title>` tag in the source. It also doesn't have a closing `</body>` tag in the source file. All of these show up under the "Elements" tab though.
- Note if you're using react, you need to do `charSet` with a capital `S` instead of `charset` where it's lowercase.
